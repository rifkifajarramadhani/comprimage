# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Comprimage is for anyone who needs to make image files smaller without handing private images to an online service. Developers are the strongest secondary audience, especially when preparing assets for websites and applications. Users typically arrive with one immediate job: compress, resize, or convert an image and leave with a visibly good result as quickly as possible.

## Product Purpose

Comprimage provides professional-grade image processing directly in the browser, with no account, upload, or server-side handling. Success means a user can produce a visibly good, substantially smaller image in one quick visit while remaining confident that the source never left their device.

## Positioning

Professional-grade image compression with minimal visible quality loss, processed entirely on your device.

## Brand Personality

Trustworthy, precise, and effortless. The product should communicate technical competence in plain language, feel calm during a potentially destructive task, and make its strongest capabilities approachable to non-specialists.

## Anti-references

Comprimage should not resemble cluttered, ad-heavy file converters; intimidating professional image editors; or flashy marketing experiences that compete with the task. It should avoid manufactured urgency, ambiguous download controls, hidden limits, and technical complexity presented without a clear user benefit.

## Design Principles

1. **Make privacy tangible.** Reinforce local, on-device processing at the moments when users choose files and download results.
2. **Protect visible quality.** Optimize for the smallest result that still looks right, not the smallest number at any cost.
3. **Keep the fast path immediate.** Sensible defaults should let anyone finish quickly, while advanced controls remain available to people who need them.
4. **Explain the advantage plainly.** Translate codecs, perceptual quality, and progressive resizing into outcomes users can understand and trust.
5. **Keep every screen focused.** Each tool should support one clear workflow with predictable controls, feedback, and completion.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Support complete keyboard operation, visible focus states, strong text and control contrast, reduced-motion preferences, and feedback that never relies on color alone. Use plain language for the primary workflow while keeping technical details available without making them prerequisites.
